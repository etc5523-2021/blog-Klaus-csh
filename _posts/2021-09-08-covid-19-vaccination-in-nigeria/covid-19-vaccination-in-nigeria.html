💡 Introduction

With the large-scale production of vaccines, many countries in the world are speeding up the vaccination process. However, some countries are unable to develop and produce vaccines by themselves due to lack of scientific research and technical support and funds, especially in poor countries. As one of the representatives, Nigeria is a country under the Humanitarian Response Plan(HRP), which requires external support. As a journalist, in this blog post I will focus on the introduction through Our World in Data about Nigeria’s vaccination situation, to help them gain more support from countries.

📕 Data Description

Two data sets are used in a brief overview of the COVID-19 situation in Nigeria, one on COVID-19 cases and vaccination, and the other on the number of vaccines obtained in Nigeria.

COVID-19 Confirmed cases, Death and Vaccination

Because Nigeria’s data is partially missing, it mainly shows the number of confirmed cases, deaths, and the number of vaccinations.

total_vaccinations Total number of doses administered. For vaccines that require multiple doses, each individual dose is counted. If a person receives one dose of the vaccine, this metric goes up by 1. If they receive a second dose, it goes up by 1 again. If they receive a third/booster dose, it goes up by 1 again.
people_vaccinated total number of people who received at least one vaccine dose. If a person receives the first dose of a 2-dose vaccine, this metric goes up by 1. If they receive the second dose, the metric stays the same.
people_fully_vaccinated Total number of people who received all doses prescribed by the vaccination protocol. If a person receives the first dose of a 2-dose vaccine, this metric stays the same. If they receive the second dose, the metric goes up by 1.
new_vaccinations Daily change in the total number of doses administered. It is only calculated for consecutive days.
new_vaccinations_smoothed New doses administered per day (7-day smoothed). For countries that don’t report data on a daily basis, we assume that doses changed equally on a daily basis over any periods in which no data was reported. This produces a complete series of daily figures, which is then averaged over a rolling 7-day window.

🌏 Information on a global scale

Percentage of the vaccinated population

Percentage of the population fully vaccinated

As of September 3, 2021, the percentage of Nigerian vaccinated population is 2.37%, and the percentage of fully vaccinated population is 0.73%, which is much lower than the world average of 30.3%. It can be seen from the map that the vaccination rate is very low throughout Africa.

Situtation on reproduce rate

Figure 3: The fluctuation of reproduce rate in Nigeria

The reproduce rate is compared with the initial data (1.25) in the Figure 3 . It can be seen that from the control of the epidemic to the beginning of the vaccination, these measures have led to a decline in the infection rate. However, due to the limited number of vaccines, and the mutation of the virus, the vaccine effect has been reduced, resulting in no significant drop in the infection rate.

🔖 Conclusion

As a front reporter, I hope to report the real situation in Nigeria so that the world can focus more on this barren country, and hope that other powerful countries can help these countries more. The elimination of the epidemic does not depend on how quickly the powerful countries can control the epidemic, but on how quickly these weak countries can overcome the difficulties (Liang and others (2020)). I hope that major countries can provide these countries with more economic, technological, and material support.
